!!!Materials / Resources Needed
   Whiteboard or projector
   Markers or pens
   Construction paper or index cards (various colors)
   Toy blocks (like LEGOs or Duplo)
   Visual aids (pictures of coins, chains, computers)
   Worksheet with simple questions about Bitcoin and Blockchain
   Optional: Computer or tablet for showing short videos

!!!Assessment / Evaluation
   Observation of student participation in discussions and activities.
   Review of completed worksheets.
   Informal questioning throughout the lesson.
   Collect toy block chain to assess understanding of chain construction.

!!!Vocabulary
   Bitcoin: A special kind of digital money.
   Blockchain: A way to keep track of information in a safe and secure way, like a special digital notebook.
   Digital: Existing or stored on a computer.
   Transaction: When someone sends or receives something.
   Block: A container of information in the blockchain.
   Chain: A series of blocks linked together.

!!!Guided Practice (10-15 minutes)
1.  Blockchain Construction: As a class, build a physical blockchain using toy blocks. Each block represents a transaction. Write a simple "transaction" on a piece of paper and tape it to a block (e.g., "Alice gives Bob 1 cookie"). Link the blocks together to create the chain. Emphasize that each block is connected to the previous one.
2.  Role-Playing: Act out a simple transaction. One student is Alice, one is Bob. Alice gives Bob a pretend "Bitcoin". Explain that this transaction is written down in a "block" on the blockchain.

!!!Closure (5 minutes)
   Review the key concepts of Bitcoin and Blockchain.
   Ask students to summarize what they learned in one sentence.
   Briefly discuss how blockchain technology can be used for other things besides money (e.g., tracking toys, verifying art).

!!!Introduction / Warm-Up (5-10 minutes)
1.  Show pictures of different kinds of money (coins, paper money, credit cards). Ask: "What is money used for?" "How do we know who has money?"
2.  Introduce Bitcoin as a special kind of digital money. "Bitcoin is like a digital coin – it lives on computers!"
3.  Ask: "How do we keep track of who has Bitcoin?" Transition to the concept of the blockchain.

!!!Independent Practice (10-15 minutes)
   Worksheet: Students complete a simple worksheet with questions about Bitcoin and Blockchain. Examples: "What is Bitcoin?" "What is a Blockchain?" "Draw a picture of a Blockchain with three blocks."
   Coloring Activity: Provide a coloring page featuring Bitcoin symbols or a simplified diagram of a blockchain.

!!!Differentiation
   For students who need more support: Provide simplified explanations and one-on-one assistance. Pre-written notes with definitions can be given.
   For students who are ready for a challenge: Encourage them to research other uses of blockchain technology (e.g., tracking food from farm to table). They can draw a picture illustrating this.

!!!Teacher Directed Activities
   Introduce the concepts of Bitcoin and Blockchain using clear and simple language.
   Lead the guided practice activity, demonstrating how to build a blockchain with toy blocks.
   Facilitate class discussions and answer student questions.
   Explain the instructions for the independent practice activity.
   Show short videos demonstrating the blockchain and Bitcoin (if technology allows).

!!!Student Directed Activities
   Participate in class discussions and answer questions.
   Build a blockchain with toy blocks during the guided practice activity.
   Role-play a simple transaction.
   Complete the worksheet or coloring activity independently.
   Optional: Research other uses of blockchain technology (if assigned).

!!!Extension, Refinement, and Practice Activities
   Create a Story: Students write a short story about using Bitcoin in the future.
   Build a More Complex Blockchain: Create a larger blockchain with more blocks and complex transactions.
   Research Blockchain Applications: Investigate how blockchain technology is used in different industries.
   Design a Digital Coin: Students design their own digital coin and create a description of its features.

!!!Use of Media & Technology
   Short videos explaining Bitcoin and Blockchain in a kid-friendly way (if available and age appropriate). Examples: Animated explainer videos.
   Visual aids: Display images of Bitcoins, blockchains, and computers.
   Interactive Whiteboard can be used to draw and illustrate the concepts.

!!!Cooperative Grouping
   Blockchain Building Teams: Divide students into small groups to build blockchains using toy blocks. Each group can focus on a different type of transaction (e.g., food, toys, books).
   Think-Pair-Share: Students think about a question related to Bitcoin or Blockchain, then share their ideas with a partner, and finally share with the whole class.
