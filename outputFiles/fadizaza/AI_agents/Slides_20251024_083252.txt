!!! What are AI Agents?
*   AI Agents are like robots with brains!
*   They can perceive (see), reason (think), and act (do).
*   Key parts:
    *   **Sensors:** How they see the world (like eyes).
    *   **Actuators:** How they do things (like hands).
    *   **Brain (AI):** How they decide what to do.
*   Example: A self-driving car uses cameras (sensors) to see the road and steering wheel (actuators) to drive. The AI decides what to do.

!!! Types of AI Agents
*   **Simple Reflex Agents:** Act based on the *current* situation only. (Like a thermostat - if too cold, turn on the heat.)
*   **Model-Based Reflex Agents:** Remember past experiences to make better decisions. (Like driving - remember that a red light means stop even if you can't see any cars.)
*   **Goal-Based Agents:** Try to achieve a specific goal. (Like a robot vacuum cleaning a room.)
*   **Utility-Based Agents:** Try to achieve the *best* result possible. (Like a chess-playing AI trying to win, not just avoid losing.)

!!! Design Your Own Agent!
*   Task: Make an AI agent that waters your plants.
*   Sensors: Soil moisture sensor (measures how wet the soil is).
*   Actuators: Water pump.
*   Decision-making: IF soil is dry, THEN turn on water pump. ELSE turn off water pump. (Simple Reflex Agent).
*   Bonus: What if we wanted a Model-Based Agent? (Remember past watering times to avoid overwatering).

!!! Agent Architectures: Simple vs. Complex
*   **Simple (Reflex):**
    *   Strength: Easy to build and fast.
    *   Weakness: Can't handle complex situations. (Example: Can't adapt to different plant types).
*   **Complex (Utility-Based):**
    *   Strength: Can handle very complex situations and optimize for the best outcome. (Example: Can consider weather, plant type, and water availability).
    *   Weakness: Harder to build and slower.
