Visual Shapes in Microsoft PowerPoint

Difficulty: Easy

Learning Outcomes:

  • Finding the "Shapes" menu
  • Drawing a square or circle
  • Clicking and dragging to move shapes

Activity: Create a Simple Shape Collage

Objective: Learn how to insert, draw, and move shapes in PowerPoint.

Open Microsoft PowerPoint

  • Click on the Start button (Windows) or Launchpad (Mac).
  • Type "Microsoft PowerPoint" and press Enter.
  • Click "Blank Presentation" to start a new slide.

Find the "Shapes" Menu

  • Look at the top menu bar. You should see tabs like Home, Insert, Design, etc.
  • Click on the "Insert" tab.
  • In the "Illustrations" group, click on "Shapes" (it looks like a small square with a curved line).
Note: If you don't see "Insert," check if you're on the correct slide.

Draw a Square

  • In the "Shapes" dropdown, find the "Rectangle" (it looks like a square).
  • Click and hold the left mouse button.
  • Move your mouse to the slide and drag to draw a square.
  • Let go when the square is about 2 inches wide.
Tip: If it's not a perfect square, try again—practice makes perfect!

Draw a Circle

  • In the "Shapes" dropdown, find the "Oval" (it looks like a circle).
  • Click and hold the left mouse button.
  • Drag to draw a circle (about 1.5 inches wide).
Tip: Hold Shift while dragging to make a perfect circle!

Move the Shapes

  • Click once on the square (it should have small handles around it).
  • Click and drag the square to a new position on the slide.
  • Repeat with the circle—move it to the bottom-right corner.
Note: If the shape doesn't move, click again to select it first!

Save Your Work

  • Click "File" (top-left corner).
  • Click "Save As".
  • Choose a location (like Desktop).
  • Name your file: "MyShapes_PowerPoint".
  • Click "Save".

Homework Task:

  • Open your saved file tomorrow.
  • Try drawing two more shapes (one triangle and one rectangle).
  • Move them to create a fun pattern (like a flag or a simple logo).
  • Save it again with a new name: "MyShapes_PowerPoint_Homework".

Bonus Challenge (Optional)

  • Can you make a heart shape? (Hint: Draw two overlapping ovals!)
  • Can you resize a shape? (Click and drag the small squares on the corners.)
Great job! You've just learned how to insert, draw, and move shapes in PowerPoint. Keep practicing—you're getting better every time!

Teacher's Note:

  • Encourage students to experiment with different shapes.
  • Remind them to save often to avoid losing work.
  • For struggling students, suggest using Shift + drag for perfect circles/squares.
