!!! Materials / Resources Needed
- Computers/tablets with Microsoft PowerPoint installed
- Projector and screen (for demonstration)
- Printed step-by-step guide (optional for struggling students)
- Colored markers or stickers (for labeling shapes on paper)
- Whiteboard and markers

!!! Assessment / Evaluation
- Observe students as they:
  - Locate the "Shapes" menu (1 point)
  - Draw a square and circle correctly (2 points each)
  - Move shapes by clicking and dragging (1 point)
- Quick verbal check: "Show me how you move a shape!"
- Exit ticket: Students draw one shape on paper and label it (e.g., "square").

!!! Vocabulary
- Shape: A 2D figure (e.g., square, circle).
- Menu: A list of options (like a dropdown list).
- Click: Press and release the mouse button.
- Drag: Click and hold to move something.
- PowerPoint: A program for creating slideshows.

!!! Introduction / Warm-Up (5-10 minutes)
1. Show pictures of squares, circles, and other shapes on the board.
2. Ask: "What shapes do you see? Can you name them?"
3. Explain: "Today, we’ll draw these shapes using PowerPoint!"
4. Demonstrate opening PowerPoint and showing the blank slide.

!!! Guided Practice (10-15 minutes)
1. Step 1: Find the Shapes Menu
   - Click the "Insert" tab (point to it on screen).
   - Show the "Shapes" dropdown arrow (click it to reveal options).
2. Step 2: Draw a Shape
   - Click "Rectangle" (for square) or "Oval" (for circle).
   - Drag on the slide to create a shape.
3. Step 3: Move a Shape
   - Click anywhere on the shape, then drag it to a new spot.
4. Repeat with 2–3 students at the front, guiding their clicks.

!!! Independent Practice (10-15 minutes)
- Task: Each student opens PowerPoint and:
  1. Draw one square and one circle (use different colors if possible).
  2. Move each shape to a different spot on the slide.
- Hint Cards: Provide visual reminders (e.g., a screenshot of the "Insert" tab).
- Support: Pair struggling students with a peer or use the printed guide.

!!! Teacher Directed Activities
- Whole Group: Live demo of drawing/moving shapes while explaining steps.
- Small Groups: Rotate to help students who need assistance with the menu or dragging.

!!! Student Directed Activities
- Individual: Complete the independent task independently.
- Choice: Let students pick a shape color or size (e.g., "Make your square big!").

!!! Differentiation
- Struggling Learners:
  - Pre-highlight the "Insert" tab on their screens.
  - Use a mouse with a larger button or a trackpad with visual guides.
  - Provide a one-page cheat sheet with icons (e.g., 🖱️ click + 👉 drag).
- Advanced Learners:
  - Challenge: "Draw a shape, then resize it by clicking the corners."
  - Extend: "Add a second shape and stack them like blocks."

!!! Extension, Refinement, and Practice Activities
- Refinement:
  - "Can you make your circle turn into a square by dragging the corners?" (Show how to resize.)
- Practice:
  - Shape Hunt: Print shapes on paper; students find the matching PowerPoint tool.
  - Storytime: "Draw a shape for your favorite animal!" (e.g., circle for a ball, square for a house).
- Homework: Draw 3 shapes and describe them in a sentence (e.g., "I drew a big blue square.").

!!! Use of Media & Technology
- Interactive Demo: Use [PowerPoint’s built-in tutorials](https://support.microsoft.com/en-us/office) (show a short video clip).
- Visual Aids: Annotate the screen with arrows to highlight the "Insert" tab and shapes menu.
- Gamification: Time students to see who can draw and move a shape fastest (fun, not graded).

!!! Cooperative Grouping
- Pairs: Buddy system—one student finds the shapes menu while the other draws.
- Groups of 4: Assign roles:
  1. Shape Artist (draws shapes).
  2. Drag Expert (moves shapes).
  3. Color Chooser (picks colors).
  4. Recorder (writes down steps on paper).
- Gallery Walk: Groups present their slides to the class (1 slide per group).

!!! Closure (5 minutes)
1. Review: Ask 3–4 students to share:
   - "How did you find the Shapes menu?"
   - "What shape did you draw first?"
2. Exit Question: "Show me how you’d move a shape using your finger (air-drag gesture)."
3. Home Connection: "Next time, try drawing a shape and telling a friend about it!"
4. High-Five Line: Students line up to high-five the teacher while saying, "I can draw shapes in PowerPoint!"
