!!! Lesson Plan: Using Pictures in Microsoft PowerPoint
Grade Level: Elementary (Ages 6–8)
Duration: 45–60 minutes
Task: Task 6 (Inserting and Resizing Pictures)

---

!!! Materials / Resources Needed
- Computers/tablets with Microsoft PowerPoint installed (1 per student or per pair)
- Pre-downloaded pictures (animals, nature, or student-friendly clipart) saved on a shared drive or USB
- Printed step-by-step visual guide (for reference)
- Whiteboard and markers
- Sticky notes (for vocabulary)
- Timer for activities
- Sample PowerPoint slide with a picture already inserted (for demonstration)

---

!!! Vocabulary
- Insert: To add something (like a picture) into a document.
- Tab: A clickable section at the top of a program (e.g., "Insert," "Home").
- Resize: To change the size of something by dragging its corners.
- Picture: An image or photo.
- Drag: To move the mouse while holding the button down.

---

!!! Introduction / Warm-Up (5–10 minutes)
- Engage: Show students a fun picture (e.g., a cartoon animal or a space scene) and ask:
  "What do you see? How could we add this to a presentation?"
- Explain: Briefly introduce the Insert tab (show a screenshot of PowerPoint’s ribbon).
  "Today, we’ll learn how to add pictures to our slides—just like adding a sticker to a notebook!"
- Demo: Open a blank PowerPoint slide and click the Insert tab together. Point out the Pictures icon.

---

!!! Guided Practice (10–15 minutes)
1. Step 1: Locate the Insert Tab
   - Teacher clicks the Insert tab on the screen. Students repeat: "Insert tab!"
   - Write "Insert" on the board with a sticky note labeled "Tab."

2. Step 2: Insert a Picture
   - Teacher clicks Pictures > This Device (or shared drive). Students watch as a picture appears.
   - Click the picture to insert it. "Now it’s on our slide!"

3. Step 3: Resize the Picture
   - Teacher clicks the picture, then drags a corner (shows 4 arrows). Students mimic:
     "Drag the corner to make it bigger or smaller!"
   - Practice resizing together 2–3 times.

4. Think-Pair-Share:
   - "Why might we resize a picture? (Too big? Too small?)"
   - Pairs discuss, then share with the class.

---

!!! Independent Practice (10–15 minutes)
- Task: Students open a new PowerPoint slide and:
  1. Click the Insert tab.
  2. Insert one picture from the shared folder.
  3. Resize it to fit the slide (teacher circulates to help).
- Scaffold: Provide a checklist:
  - [ ] Found the Insert tab.
  - [ ] Added a picture.
  - [ ] Resized the picture.

---

!!! Assessment / Evaluation
- Formative:
  - Observe students during guided practice (can they locate the Insert tab and resize?).
  - Check independent work for:
    - Picture inserted correctly (✅/❌).
    - Picture resized (even if just slightly).
- Exit Ticket (3 minutes):
  - Ask students to hold up:
    - 1 finger if they inserted a picture.
    - 2 fingers if they also resized it.

---

!!! Teacher Directed Activities
- Direct Instruction:
  - Live demo of inserting/resizing with think-aloud: "First, I click Insert... then I look for Pictures..."
- Guided Questions:
  - "What happens if you drag the top corner?" (Answer: Stretches the picture.)
  - "Where do we go to add a picture?" (Answer: Insert tab.)

---

!!! Student Directed Activities
- Independent Task: Insert and resize a picture (as above).
- Choice Board (for early finishers):
  - Add a second picture.
  - Move the picture to a different spot.
  - Try inserting a picture from the internet (if time allows).

---

!!! Differentiation
- Support:
  - Provide a visual step-by-step guide with arrows.
  - Pair struggling students with a peer who finished early.
  - Use larger pictures for easier dragging.
- Extension:
  - Challenge students to crop a picture (using the crop tool).
  - Have them describe their picture to a partner ("My picture is a dog. It’s big/small...").

---

!!! Cooperative Grouping
- Pairs: During independent practice, students work in pairs to:
  1. Take turns inserting a picture.
  2. Take turns resizing it.
- Role Assignment:
  - Leader: Finds the Insert tab.
  - Helper: Drags the picture corners.

---
!!! Use of Media & Technology
- Interactive Demo: Use a projector to show real-time clicks and resizing.
- Pre-Saved Pictures: Ensure all images are student-friendly (no complex backgrounds).
- Screen Recording: Record a short video of the steps for review later.

---
!!! Extension, Refinement, and Practice Activities
- Refinement:
  - Error Correction: If a student inserts a picture but forgets to resize, guide them: "Let’s make it smaller so it fits!"
- Extension:
  - Storytelling Slide: Insert 2 pictures and describe a story using them.
  - Class Book: Combine slides into a class "Picture Book" about favorite animals/places.
- Practice:
  - Daily Warm-Up: Start class with "Today, we’ll add a new picture to our slide!"

---
!!! Closure (5 minutes)
- Recap:
  - Teacher points to the board: "Today, we learned to:
    1. Find the Insert tab.
    2. Add a picture.
    3. Resize it by dragging!"
- Exit Reflection:
  - "Show me your picture! What did you resize it to look like?" (Students hold up slides.)
- Home Connection:
  - "Ask a grown-up to help you add a picture to a family presentation!"

---
!!! Teacher Notes
- Troubleshooting:
  - If pictures don’t appear: Check if the folder is shared or USB is plugged in.
  - If resizing is tricky: Use Ctrl + T (Windows) or Cmd + T (Mac) to toggle handles.
- Time Management:
  - Keep guided practice under 15 minutes to allow more independent time.
- Safety:
  - Remind students not to insert pictures with personal info (e.g., faces).
