!!! Materials / Resources Needed
- Computers/tablets with Microsoft PowerPoint installed
- Pre-downloaded pictures (e.g., animals, nature, or student-drawn images) saved on the computer
- Printed step-by-step guide (visuals only, no text)
- Whiteboard and markers
- Sticky notes (for vocabulary)
- Lined paper for notes

---

!!! Assessment / Evaluation
- Formative: Observe students as they insert pictures and resize them. Check if they:
  - Locate the "Insert" tab correctly.
  - Drag corners to resize without distorting.
  - Follow verbal instructions without prompts.
- Exit Ticket: Each student saves a slide with one picture inserted and resized. Teacher reviews 3–5 slides to confirm understanding.

---

!!! Vocabulary
- Insert: To add something (like a picture) into a document.
- Tab: A clickable section at the top of a program (e.g., "Insert," "Design").
- Resize: To change the size of an object (like dragging corners).
- Drag: To click and move an object with the mouse.
- Picture: An image (e.g., a photo or drawing).

(Write words on sticky notes and place them around the room for students to find and discuss.)

---

!!! Introduction / Warm-Up (5–10 minutes)
1. Show a Slide: Display a blank PowerPoint slide with a big question mark.
2. Think-Pair-Share:
   - "What do you think we’ll do today with this slide?" (Let students guess: "Add pictures!")
3. Demo: Show a 30-second clip of inserting a picture (no sound). Ask: "Where did I click first?" (Answer: "Insert tab!")
4. Vocabulary Review: Point to sticky notes and have students repeat words aloud.

---

!!! Guided Practice (10–15 minutes)
1. Step-by-Step Demo:
   - Open a new PowerPoint slide.
   - Click the "Insert" tab (point to it and say: "This is our magic tab!").
   - Click "Pictures" > "This Device" > Select a pre-downloaded image.
   - Drag the bottom-right corner to resize (say: "Like stretching a balloon!").
2. Think-Aloud: Explain mistakes aloud (e.g., "Oops! I clicked the wrong corner—now it’s squished!").
3. Guided Attempt:
   - Students follow along on their computers while teacher circulates to help.
   - Use a visual guide (printed steps) for non-readers.

---

!!! Teacher Directed Activities
- Mini-Lesson: Directly model inserting a picture and resizing it 2–3 times with exaggerated gestures.
- Choral Response: After each step, have students repeat: "Click Insert tab!" or "Drag the corner!"
- Error Correction: If a student struggles, ask: "Where do we go first?" (Guide them to the "Insert" tab.)

---

!!! Student Directed Activities
- Independent Practice: Students insert one picture of their choice and resize it to fit a specific space (e.g., "Make it as big as your hand").
- Choice Board: Provide 3 options:
  1. Insert a picture of their favorite animal.
  2. Insert a picture and resize it to match a given size (e.g., "Like a quarter sheet of paper").
  3. Insert a picture and resize it to fill half the slide.

---

!!! Independent Practice (10–15 minutes)
- Task: Each student creates one slide with:
  - A picture inserted from the pre-downloaded folder.
  - The picture resized (no distortion).
- Scaffold: Provide a checklist with pictures:
  ![ ] Found the Insert tab
  ![ ] Added a picture
  ![ ] Resized it properly
- Support: Offer a "Help Button" (a printed card with step-by-step icons) for students who need it.

---

!!! Cooperative Grouping
- Pairs: Students work in buddy pairs to:
  1. Take turns inserting a picture.
  2. Take turns resizing it.
  3. High-five when both steps are done!
- Roles: Assign roles to avoid confusion:
  - "You’re the Clicker!" (Finds the Insert tab.)
  - "You’re the Draggler!" (Resizes the picture.)

---

!!! Use of Media & Technology
- Interactive Demo: Use a document camera to show the teacher’s screen while guiding students.
- Video Clip: Show a 1-minute silent video of inserting a picture (no sound) for visual learners.
- Accessibility: Allow students to use trackpad gestures (drag with two fingers) if needed.

---

!!! Extension, Refinement, and Practice Activities
- Challenge: Add a second picture and resize both to fit side by side.
- Creativity: Insert a picture and "turn it into a story" by adding 1–2 words below it (e.g., "My dog’s name is...").
- Game: "Picture Rescue" – Teacher calls out an object (e.g., "a tree"), and students race to insert a picture of it.

---
!!! Closure (5 minutes)
1. Gallery Walk: Students hold up their slides and say: "I inserted a picture of [X] and made it [big/small]!"
2. Exit Question: "Where do you go to add a picture in PowerPoint?" (Collect responses on sticky notes for the board.)
3. Exit Ticket: Each student saves their slide and hands in a printed screenshot (or shows the teacher their screen).

---
!!! Differentiation
- For Struggling Learners:
  - Provide a picture-only step-by-step guide (no text).
  - Pair with a peer who is confident.
  - Allow them to insert a pre-selected picture (e.g., only animals).
- For Advanced Learners:
  - Challenge them to crop the picture (remove extra edges).
  - Have them insert a picture and rotate it (extra step).
  - Let them add a border to their picture.

---
!!! Teacher Notes
- Common Misconceptions:
  - Students might click the wrong corner to resize (e.g., top-left instead of bottom-right). Remind them: "Only the bottom-right corner stretches it nicely!"
  - They may forget to save their work. Set a timer for 2 minutes before closing.
- Time-Saver: Pre-download pictures onto each student’s desktop with a folder labeled "PowerPoint Pictures."
- Engagement Tip: Play upbeat music during independent practice to keep energy high.
