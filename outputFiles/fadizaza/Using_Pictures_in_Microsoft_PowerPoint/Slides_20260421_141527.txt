**!!! Inserting Pictures in PowerPoint**
- **Why use pictures?**
  - Makes slides more interesting (like adding a photo to a storybook!).
  - Helps explain ideas faster than words alone.
- **Where to find the "Insert" tab?**
  - Look at the top of PowerPoint—it’s the **first tab** (like the start of a race!).
  - Click it to find the **"Pictures"** button (📷).

---

**!!! Finding & Inserting a Picture**
- **How to insert a picture from your computer:**
  1. Click **"Insert"** → **"Pictures"** (📷).
  2. A window opens—**browse** to where your picture is saved (like finding a toy in a box).
  3. **Double-click** the picture to insert it!
- **Tip:** Save pictures in a **folder** (e.g., "My PowerPoint Pics") to find them easily.

---

**!!! Resizing a Picture (Drag & Drop)**
- **Why resize?**
  - Too big? Too small? Adjust it to fit your slide (like trimming a photo to fit a frame!).
- **How to resize:**
  1. Click the picture to **select** it (small squares appear at corners).
  2. **Drag** any corner **inward** (to shrink) or **outward** (to grow).
  - **Pro Tip:** Hold **Shift** while dragging to keep the picture **square** (like a perfect square cookie!).

---

**!!! Picture Placement Tips**
- **Where to place?**
  - Center it (like a poster on a wall) or align it with text for clarity.
- **Quick fixes:**
  - **Rotate:** Click the picture → drag the **circular arrow** (↻) to turn it.
  - **Move:** Click and **drag** the picture like a puzzle piece.

---
**!!! Practice Time! (Try It Yourself!)**
- **Your turn!**
  1. Open PowerPoint → Click **"Insert"** → Find a picture on your computer.
  2. Insert it → **Resize** it to fit your slide (drag corners!).
  3. **Bonus:** Try rotating it or moving it to the side!
- **Remember:** Mistakes are okay—PowerPoint lets you **undo** (Ctrl+Z) anytime! 🎯
