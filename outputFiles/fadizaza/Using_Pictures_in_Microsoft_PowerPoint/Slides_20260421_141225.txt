Here are your 5 engaging PowerPoint slides for beginner students:

---

**Slide 1: !!! Welcome to Using Pictures in PowerPoint**
- **Why use pictures?**
  - Pictures make slides more interesting (like adding a photo to a poster).
  - They help explain ideas faster than words alone.
  - Example: Instead of writing "sunset," show a sunset picture!

- **Where to start?**
  - Open PowerPoint → You’ll see a blank slide (like a clean canvas).
  - Look for the **"Insert"** tab at the top (it’s like the "Add" button on a toolbox).

---

**Slide 2: !!! Finding the "Insert" Tab**
- **Where is it?**
  - At the top of PowerPoint, you’ll see tabs like **Home**, **Insert**, **Design**, etc.
  - Click **"Insert"** to add pictures, shapes, or videos.

- **What’s inside the "Insert" tab?**
  - **Pictures**: To add images from your computer.
  - **Shapes**: For arrows, boxes, or stars.
  - **Icons**: Small symbols (like a 📁 for "folder").

- **Try it!**
  - Click "Insert" → Hover over "Pictures" to see options.
  - *Analogy*: Think of "Insert" as a magic door to add cool stuff!

---

**Slide 3: !!! Inserting a Picture from Your Computer**
- **Step 1: Click "Insert" → "Pictures"**
  - A window will pop up (like opening a photo album).

- **Step 2: Find your picture**
  - Navigate to where you saved it (e.g., "Downloads" or "Pictures").
  - *Tip*: Use the search bar if you’re unsure!

- **Step 3: Click "Insert"**
  - The picture will appear on your slide (like placing a sticker).

- **Example:**
  - Want to show a cat? Click "Insert" → "Pictures" → Find a cat photo → Insert!

---

**Slide 4: !!! Resizing Pictures (Dragging Corners)**
- **Why resize?**
  - Big pictures take up too much space (like a giant poster blocking your view).
  - Small pictures might be hard to see.

- **How to resize:**
  1. Click the picture → **4 corner handles** (small squares) will appear.
  2. Drag any corner **inward** to shrink or **outward** to grow.
  3. *Tip*: Hold **Shift** while dragging to keep the picture square (like a perfect square photo).

- **Try it!**
  - Insert a picture → Drag a corner to make it tiny or huge.
  - *Analogy*: Resizing is like stretching or shrinking a rubber band!

---

**Slide 5: !!! Quick Recap & Practice**
- **What you learned:**
  - Find the **"Insert"** tab (top menu).
  - Add pictures from your computer (like adding a photo to a scrapbook).
  - Resize by dragging corners (like adjusting a poster size).

- **Your turn!**
  1. Open PowerPoint → Click "Insert" → Add a picture.
  2. Drag a corner to make it small or big.
  3. *Bonus*: Try moving the picture by clicking and dragging!

- **Remember:**
  - Pictures make slides **fun and clear**!
  - Practice makes perfect—keep experimenting! 🎨
