!!!
Lesson Plan: Using Pictures in Microsoft PowerPoint Presentations

!!!
Lesson Title: Adding Pictures to PowerPoint Slides
Grade Level: Elementary (Ages 6-9)
Duration: 45 minutes

---

!!!
Materials / Resources Needed:
- Computers with Microsoft PowerPoint installed
- Pre-downloaded pictures (animals, objects, or simple scenes) saved on the desktop
- Printed step-by-step guide (visuals for visual learners)
- Whiteboard & markers
- Sticky notes (for quick feedback)
- Sample PowerPoint file with a blank slide

---

!!!
Assessment / Evaluation:
- Formative: Observe students as they complete tasks (e.g., locating the "Insert" tab, resizing pictures).
- Checklist: Students mark off steps as they complete them (e.g., "I inserted a picture ✔️").
- Exit Ticket: Students show the teacher their slide with a picture resized correctly to exit.
- Task 6 Completion: All students finish inserting and resizing a picture by the end of the lesson.

---

!!!
Vocabulary:
- Picture: An image or drawing.
- Insert: To add something (like a picture) into a document.
- Resize: To change the size of something (e.g., dragging corners).
- Tab: A section or button in a program (e.g., "Insert" tab).
- Slide: A single page in a PowerPoint presentation.

---

!!!
Introduction / Warm-Up (5-10 minutes):
- Engage: Ask, "What’s one thing you’d like to show in a slideshow?" (e.g., a pet, favorite toy).
- Demo: Show a simple PowerPoint slide with a picture. Ask, "How do you think we got this picture here?"
- Explain: "Today, we’ll learn how to add pictures to our slides and make them bigger or smaller!"
- Preview: Show the 3 steps for today: 1) Find the "Insert" tab, 2) Add a picture, 3) Resize it.

---

!!!
Guided Practice (10-15 minutes):
1. Locate the "Insert" Tab:
   - Open a blank PowerPoint slide.
   - Point to the top ribbon and say, "This is the ‘Insert’ tab—it’s where we add pictures!"
   - Click it together as a class.

2. Insert a Picture:
   - Click the "Pictures" button (camera icon).
   - Browse to the desktop and select a pre-downloaded picture (e.g., a cat).
   - Click "Insert" and watch it appear on the slide.

3. Resize the Picture:
   - Place fingers on the bottom-right corner of the picture.
   - Drag outward to make it bigger (or inward to make it smaller).
   - Praise: "Great job stretching the picture!"

4. Troubleshooting:
   - If a picture is too big/small, demonstrate clicking "Crop" (scissors icon) to adjust edges.

---
Teacher Directed Activities:
- Model each step slowly with think-alouds ("First, I click ‘Insert’ because…").
- Use a document camera to show the screen for all students.

---

!!!
Independent Practice (10-15 minutes):
- Task 6 (Goal): Each student will:
  1. Open a new PowerPoint slide.
  2. Insert one picture from the desktop (e.g., a flower, dinosaur).
  3. Resize it to fit the slide (e.g., make it as big as their hand).
- Support: Provide a checklist:
  - [ ] Found the "Insert" tab.
  - [ ] Clicked "Pictures."
  - [ ] Dragged corners to resize.
- Differentiation: Give advanced students a second picture to insert and resize.

---
Differentiation:
- Struggling Learners: Pair with a peer or use a pre-selected picture (e.g., "Insert the red apple").
- Advanced Learners: Add a second picture and align them (e.g., side by side).
- Visual Learners: Provide a color-coded step guide (e.g., red for "Insert," blue for "Resize").

---

!!!
Cooperative Grouping:
- Pairs: Students work with a partner to insert one picture each and take turns resizing.
- Roles: One clicks "Insert," the other resizes (switch roles after one task).
- Share: Groups show their slides and say, "We added a [picture] and made it [size]!"

---

!!!
Use of Media & Technology:
- Interactive Demo: Use a tool like [PowerPoint’s "Tell Me What You Want to Do"](https://support.microsoft.com/en-us/office/tell-me-what-you-want-to-do-in-powerpoint-6270382f-1a3d-4478-9f2f-499726d02a90) to show voice-guided steps.
- Video Clip: Show a 1-minute video of resizing (e.g., [How to Resize in PowerPoint](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=example)).
- Screen Recording: Students record themselves inserting a picture (optional for review).

---

!!!
Extension, Refinement, and Practice Activities:
- Refinement: Add a border to the picture using the "Shape Outline" tool (for advanced).
- Extension:
  - Task 7: Insert 2 pictures and arrange them (e.g., a cat under a tree).
  - Creative Task: Make a "My Favorite Things" slide with 3 pictures.
- Practice: Play "Picture Detective"—teacher describes a picture (e.g., "a blue whale"), and students insert it.

---

!!!
Closure (5 minutes):
- Review: Ask, "What’s one thing you learned today?" (Call on 3-4 students.)
- Exit Ticket: Hold up a thumb:
  - Up: I inserted and resized a picture!
  - Sideways: I need more help with resizing.
- Homework: Practice inserting a picture at home (e.g., a family photo).

---
Teacher Notes:
- Time Check: If independent practice runs long, shorten closure to 3 minutes.
- Task 6 Focus: Ensure all students complete inserting and resizing by the end.
- Tech Tip: Save files as "StudentName_Pictures.pptx" to track progress.
