**Slide 1: !!! Welcome to Using Pictures in PowerPoint**
- Adding pictures makes your slides **more interesting** like adding a poster to a bulletin board!
- Today, you’ll learn how to **insert, resize, and place** pictures easily.
- **Why use pictures?**
  - Helps explain ideas faster (like a visual story).
  - Keeps your audience engaged (like a movie poster).

---

**Slide 2: !!! Finding the "Insert" Tab**
- The **"Insert" tab** is your **picture toolbox**—it’s where you add images, shapes, and more!
- **Where to find it:**
  - Look at the top of PowerPoint (like a menu).
  - Click the **"Insert"** tab (it has icons like a picture, shape, and chart).
- **Example:** Think of it like a **toolbox**—you open it to pick the right tool (here, the "Picture" tool).

---

**Slide 3: !!! Inserting a Picture from Your Computer**
- **Step 1:** Click the **"Insert"** tab → Find the **"Pictures"** icon (looks like a photo).
- **Step 2:** A window will open—**browse** your computer like searching for a file.
- **Step 3:** Select your picture (e.g., a photo of a cat) → Click **"Insert"**.
- **Tip:** Save pictures in a **folder** (like a "Pictures for School" folder) to find them fast!

---

**Slide 4: !!! Resizing a Picture (Drag the Corners!)**
- **Why resize?** To make your picture fit the slide (like trimming a poster to fit a wall).
- **How to do it:**
  1. Click the picture to **select** it (it gets a blue border).
  2. Look at the **four corners**—they have small **black squares**.
  3. **Drag** (click and hold) a corner to **stretch** the picture bigger or smaller.
- **Pro Tip:** Hold **Shift** while dragging to keep the picture **square** (like a perfect photo frame).

---
**Slide 5: !!! Practice Time! (Try It Yourself!)**
- **Your Turn!**
  1. Open PowerPoint → Click **"Insert"** tab.
  2. Insert a picture (use one from your "Pictures" folder).
  3. Resize it by dragging the corners—make it **smaller** or **bigger**!
- **Challenge:** Can you resize it to fit **half the slide**?
