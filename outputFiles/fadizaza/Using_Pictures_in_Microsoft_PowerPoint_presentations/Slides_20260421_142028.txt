**!!! Inserting Pictures in PowerPoint**

- **Why use pictures?**
  - Pictures make slides **more interesting** than just text.
  - Example: Instead of saying *"This is a dog,"* show a picture of a dog!
  - Helps your audience **remember** what you’re teaching.

---

**!!! Finding the "Insert" Tab**

- **Where is it?**
  - At the top of PowerPoint, look for a tab labeled **"Insert"** (it has a plus sign ✚).
  - Think of it like a **"toolbox"** for adding things to your slide.

- **What’s inside?**
  - Pictures, shapes, charts, and more!
  - Example: Want to add a photo? Click **"Pictures"** here.

---

**!!! How to Insert a Picture from Your Computer**

- **Step 1:** Click **"Insert"** → **"Pictures"** (or press *Ctrl+G*).
- **Step 2:** A window opens—**browse** your computer like a treasure hunt!
  - Find the picture you want (e.g., a family photo or a fun meme).
- **Step 3:** Click **"Insert"** to add it to your slide.

- **Tip:** If the picture is too big, **don’t panic**—we’ll resize it next!

---

**!!! Resizing a Picture (Drag the Corners!)**

- **How to make it bigger/smaller:**
  - Click the picture to **select** it (it gets a blue border).
  - Look at the **four corners**—they have little **black squares**.
  - **Drag** any corner (like pulling a rubber band) to resize!

- **Example:**
  - Too big? Drag inward (like shrinking a balloon).
  - Too small? Drag outward (like blowing up a balloon).

- **Pro Tip:** Hold *Shift* while dragging to keep it **square** (no stretching!).

---

- **Your turn!**
  1. Open PowerPoint and go to the **"Insert"** tab.
  2. Find a picture on your computer (even a screenshot works!).
  3. Insert it and **resize** it by dragging the corners.
  4. Share with a partner: *"What picture did you add?"*

- **Remember:**
  - Pictures make slides **fun and clear**!
  - Always **resize** to fit your slide like a puzzle piece.
