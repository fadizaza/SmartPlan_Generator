!!! Materials / Resources Needed
- Computers/tablets with Microsoft PowerPoint installed
- Pre-downloaded pictures (animals, objects, or student-drawn images) saved on the computer
- Printed step-by-step guide (optional visual aid)
- Whiteboard & markers
- Sticky notes (for quick reference)

---

!!! Assessment / Evaluation
- Formative: Observe students inserting pictures, resizing, and identifying the "Insert" tab.
- Checklist: Students complete a 3-step task (insert, resize, save) correctly.
- Exit Ticket: Ask 2 students to share one thing they learned about inserting pictures.

---

!!! Vocabulary
- Slide: A single page in a PowerPoint presentation.
- Insert Tab: The toolbar where you add pictures, shapes, or text.
- Resize: To change the size of a picture by dragging corners.
- Drag: To move the mouse while holding the left button to select or move an object.

---

!!! Introduction / Warm-Up (5-10 minutes)
1. Show a blank PowerPoint slide on the board.
2. Ask: "What do you think we can add to make it interesting?" (Let students guess: pictures, colors, etc.)
3. Explain: "Today, we’ll learn how to add pictures to slides—just like decorating a poster!"
4. Show a simple example of a slide with a picture inserted and resized.

---

!!! Guided Practice (10-15 minutes)
1. Step 1: Find the "Insert" Tab
   - Click the "Insert" tab (point to it on the screen).
   - Say: "This is where we add pictures!"
2. Step 2: Insert a Picture
   - Click "Pictures" → "This Device" → Select a pre-downloaded image.
   - Drag the picture onto the slide.
3. Step 3: Resize the Picture
   - Click the picture → Drag corner handles (small squares) to make it bigger/smaller.
   - Say: "Like stretching a rubber band!"
4. Demo Together: Do one slide as a class, then let 2 students try at the front.

---

!!! Independent Practice (10-15 minutes)
Task 6 (Due by End of Lesson):
1. Open a new PowerPoint slide.
2. Insert one picture from the pre-downloaded folder.
3. Resize it to fit the slide (no smaller than 2 inches tall).
4. Save the file as "MyPictureSlide" in the classroom folder.
5. Raise your hand when done!

---
!!! Closure (5 minutes)
1. Ask: "Why do we use pictures in presentations?" (Accept answers like "to make it fun" or "to show ideas").
2. Review steps:
   - Click Insert → Pictures → Resize corners.
3. Highlight 2 students who completed Task 6 correctly. Praise their work!

---
!!! Teacher Directed Activities
- Whole-group demo of inserting/resizing.
- Quick quiz: "Where do we click to add a picture?" (Hold up "Insert" tab card).
- Circulate to help students with Task 6.

---
!!! Student Directed Activities
- Inserting their own picture (from pre-downloaded folder).
- Experimenting with resizing (encourage trying different sizes).
- Saving their file with the correct name.

---
!!! Differentiation
- Support: Provide a printed step-by-step guide for visual learners.
- Challenge: Ask advanced students to insert 2 pictures and arrange them neatly.
- Kinesthetic: Let students use a mouse with a large button for easier dragging.

---
!!! Extension, Refinement, and Practice Activities
- Refinement: Let students practice inserting different types of pictures (e.g., clipart vs. photos).
- Extension: Add a task: "Resize your picture to match the slide’s width."
- Practice: Play a "Picture Hunt" game—students find and insert a hidden picture in 1 minute.

---
!!! Use of Media & Technology
- Interactive Whiteboard: Show enlarged steps for resizing.
- Screen Sharing: Project a student’s screen to model correct resizing.
- Pre-Saved Pictures: Use simple, recognizable images (e.g., a cat, a tree).

---
!!! Cooperative Grouping
- Pairs: Students work in 2s to insert one picture each and share screens.
- Roles: Assign one student to insert the picture, another to resize it.
- Gallery Walk: After completing Task 6, students rotate to admire each other’s slides.
