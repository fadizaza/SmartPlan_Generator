!!! Materials / Resources Needed

 Computers with internet access and OneDrive accounts (student accounts can be created beforehand, or use teacher-created demonstration accounts).
 Projector or interactive whiteboard to display OneDrive interface.
 Printed handouts with simple instructions and images of the OneDrive interface (optional).
 Prepared sample files (pictures, documents) for uploading and downloading.
 Chart paper or whiteboard for vocabulary.

!!! Instructional Steps / Procedure

1. Introduction/Warm-up (5-10 minutes): Begin with a discussion about saving files, where we keep our important work, and introduce the concept of cloud storage.
2. Teacher-Directed Activities (15-20 minutes): Introduce OneDrive, show its interface, and explain key features like folders, files, and the search bar.  Demonstrate uploading a file, downloading a file, and sharing a file (different share options – link vs. specific people). Create a shared folder together.
3. Guided Practice (10-15 minutes):  Students, with teacher guidance, upload a picture or document they have already saved on their computer, then download it to a different folder.
4. Independent Practice (10-15 minutes): Students independently create a folder, name it, add a file, and share it with the teacher (or a designated classmate).
5. Student-Directed Activities (10 minutes):  Students explore OneDrive’s interface on their own, experimenting with searching for files and navigating folders.
6. Closure (5 minutes): Review key concepts learned and address any outstanding questions.

!!! Assessment / Evaluation

 Observation during guided and independent practice.
 Checking student-created folders and shared files.
 Simple quiz on key vocabulary and functionality (optional).

!!! Guided Practice (10-15 minutes)

Students upload a pre-selected image (provided by teacher) to their OneDrive and download it to a new folder they create. Teacher provides individual assistance as needed.

!!! Closure (5 minutes)

Quick review of steps:  Upload, Download, Share, Folder creation.  Teacher asks students to summarize what they learned in their own words.

!!! Introduction / Warm-Up (5-10 minutes)

Start with a discussion about where students keep their drawings or important papers. Introduce the idea of a digital space to save files and that it's like having a special folder in the "cloud".

!!! Independent Practice (10-15 minutes)

Students create a folder, add a file (picture or document), and share it with the teacher (or pre-selected classmate) using OneDrive’s sharing features.


!!! Differentiation

 For students who need more support: Pair them with a more advanced peer for assistance. Provide simpler instructions with more visual aids.
 For students who are ready for a challenge:  Have them explore advanced sharing options (permissions) or investigate OneDrive's recycle bin.


!!! Teacher Directed Activities

 Introducing OneDrive interface and features via a screencast or live demonstration.
 Step-by-step instructions on uploading, downloading, and sharing files.
 Guided creation of a shared folder.


!!! Student Directed Activities

 Independent exploration of OneDrive's interface.
 Creating and sharing files and folders.
 Troubleshooting simple synchronization problems (with teacher support).


!!! Extension, Refinement, and Practice Activities

 Research other cloud storage services (Google Drive, Dropbox).
 Create a presentation about OneDrive's features.
 Design a poster explaining how to use OneDrive safely and effectively.


!!! Use of Media & Technology

 Screencasting software to demonstrate OneDrive functions.
 Interactive whiteboard to annotate the OneDrive interface.
 Computers with internet access for student use.


!!! Cooperative Groupings

 Students can work in pairs during guided practice and independent practice for peer support and collaboration.  Larger groups can be used for extension activities where students collaboratively create a presentation or poster.
